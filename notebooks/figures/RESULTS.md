Across 8 secondary-substation feeders, the day-ahead forecast benchmark gives an aggregate MAPE of 36.18% for seasonal-naive, 40.92% for SARIMAX and 30.51% for the interpretable structured model; the best aggregate MAPE is 30.51%. The structured model beats the seasonal-naive baseline (30.51% vs 36.18%); it stays above the <5% target (gap 25.51 pp) because single-feeder half-hourly demand is far noisier than the aggregated load that target was set for.

Federating the structured model across 8 substation nodes over 12 rounds moves the aggregate test MAPE from 30.79% (local-only) to 23.32% (federated-global), vs 22.99% for a centralised model trained on pooled data — without any raw data leaving a node. The thin-history feeder (only 24 training rows) goes from 111.02% local to 49.24% under federation, a cold-start change of 61.78 pp.
