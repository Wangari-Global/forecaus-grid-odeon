At the secondary-substation-total level — the granularity the challenge targets — the day-ahead benchmark across 6 substations gives an aggregate MAPE of 35.45% for seasonal-naive, 39.79% for SARIMAX and 29.81% for the interpretable structured model, at 0.906 interval coverage. The structured model beats the seasonal-naive baseline (29.81% vs 35.45%). It stays above the <5% target (gap 24.81 pp) because these substations roll up only a handful of LV feeders (tens of homes), so they are still far spikier than whole-network load — the level that target was set for; much larger aggregation would be needed to approach it.

At the harder per-feeder level (8 feeders, a lower bound) the structured model gives 30.51% MAPE versus 36.18% for seasonal-naive and 40.92% for SARIMAX.

Federating the structured model across 6 substation nodes (one SS-total series each) over 12 rounds moves the aggregate test MAPE from 33.83% (local-only) to 23.85% (federated-global), vs 23.51% for a centralised model trained on pooled data — without any raw data leaving a node (only parameter vectors are exchanged). The thin-history substation (only 24 training rows) goes from 111.02% local to 49.95% under federation, a cold-start change of 61.07 pp.
